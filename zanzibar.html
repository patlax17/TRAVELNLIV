Day-by-Day

Your 8-Day Journey

Day 1

Welcome to Zanzibar

Stone Town · 5★ Hotel

Once you've arrived, cleared the airport and checked into your Stone Town hotel, take some time to settle in and soak up your first taste of the island. Enjoy the hotel facilities, explore the surroundings or simply unwind. In the evening, we'll come together for our welcome dinner — the perfect chance to meet the group and officially kick off the adventure.

🛬 Airport Transfer 🏨 Hotel Check-in 🍽️ Welcome Dinner
Day 2

Spice Farm & Stone Town

Stone Town · Culture Day

Start the morning with our welcome breakfast, a relaxed moment to fuel up and connect with the group. Afterwards, we head out for a guided spice farm experience — learning about Zanzibar's rich spice heritage. In the afternoon, we explore the historic Stone Town, visiting cultural sites, museums and local markets.

🌿 Spice Farm Tour 🏛️ Stone Town Walk 🧳 Local Markets
Day 3

Prison Island & Nakupenda Sandbank

Indian Ocean · Water Day

After breakfast, we take a traditional wooden dhow boat to Changuu (Prison) Island for a guided tour and an up-close encounter with the famous giant tortoises. From there, we continue to the beautiful Nakupenda Sandbank for a beachside BBQ lunch and crystal-clear water relaxation.

⛵ Dhow Boat 🐢 Giant Tortoises 🏖️ Sandbank BBQ
Day 4

Move to Kiwengwa Beach

Kiwengwa · Villa Resort

Today we transition to Zanzibar's stunning northeastern coast. After breakfast and check-out, we transfer from Stone Town to our beachfront resort in Kiwengwa. Once you've settled into your villa, the rest of the day is yours to enjoy at leisure — ocean views, resort pool, total freedom.

🚐 Resort Transfer 🌊 Villa Check-in 🏊 Leisure Day
Day 5

Jozani Forest & Asalaam Cave

Kiwengwa · Nature Adventure

After a chilled breakfast, we head out for a nature-filled adventure. First is a visit to Jozani National Park, home to Zanzibar's famous red colobus monkeys, for a guided forest walk. Afterwards, we continue to Asalaam Cave for the unique experience of swimming with turtles.

🌿 Jozani Forest 🐒 Red Colobus Monkeys 🐢 Swim with Turtles
Day 6

Mnemba Island Catamaran

Mnemba Island · Ocean Day

Today we head out to explore the stunning reefs of Mnemba Island. A full-day snorkelling experience takes us into some of Zanzibar's most pristine waters — known for vibrant marine life and crystal-clear conditions. Watch for colourful reef fish and, if we're lucky, playful dolphins.

⛵ Catamaran Cruise 🤿 Snorkelling 🐬 Dolphins (maybe!)
Day 7

Leisure Day & Farewell Dinner

Kiwengwa · Villa Resort

Today is a free leisure day to enjoy at your own pace — beach time, poolside lounging, spa moments, or simply switching off. In the evening, we come together one last time for our farewell dinner: a chance to dress up, celebrate the trip, and toast to new friendships.

🏖️ Free Day 💆 Spa Time 🥂 Farewell Dinner
Day 8

Departure Day

Zanzibar Airport · ZNZ

After breakfast and a final slow morning in paradise, it's time to check out and prepare for your transfer to Zanzibar Airport. We hope you're leaving with new friendships, unforgettable memories, and a camera roll full of pure enjoyment.

🍳 Final Breakfast 🛬 Airport Transfer ✈️ Depart ZNZ

No — flights are not included as part of the package. You'll need to book your own flight to Zanzibar Abeid Amani Karume International Airport (ZNZ). We recommend Kayak or Skyscanner for the best deals. Please land in Zanzibar before 12pm on Day 1 to be eligible for the airport transfer.

Most foreign passport holders need a tourist visa to enter Zanzibar. You can apply for an eVisa in advance at visa.immigration.go.tz. Visa on arrival is available for British passport holders and some other countries. Please note we cannot assist with visa applications.

Yes — travel insurance is a must. Make sure it covers water-related activities. The Zanzibar government also requires all visitors to have inbound travel insurance (ZIC), costing USD $44 per person. This covers some medical emergencies and travel disruptions and can be purchased at inbound.visitzanzibar.go.tz.

Both villa types offer the same interior standard, size and comfort. The difference is location and outlook. Garden View Villas are set within the resort's lush tropical grounds, while Beachfront Villas are positioned closer to the shoreline with direct sea-facing views.
